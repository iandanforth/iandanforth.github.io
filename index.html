Research Interests

Motor Unit Control - Inspired by Daniel Wolpert's movement chauvinism I want to build systems that learn to control muscles. I work with simulated agents rigged with muscles at various levels of fidelity.

Learned Reward Signals - How do we augment reinforcement learning to overcome the reward shaping problem? What are the mechanisms by which an agent can learn what should constitute a reward and what should not. I approach this problem from the perspective of operant conditioning and ask what systems must be implemented in an agent to produce behavior similar to that in biological systems under the control of various schedules of reinforcement.

Curriculum Learning - How do we progressively modify an agent's environment such that tasks with otherwise sparse reward signals can be learned efficiently?

Lifelong Learning - How do we accomplish all of the above without suffering from catastrophic forgetting?
